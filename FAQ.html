Frequently Asked Questions

Descent is a space shooter game originally released for MS-DOS (Descent 1 & 2), early Windows systems like Windows 98 or XP (Descent 2 & 3) and Playstation (Descent). The Playstation version is called Descent Maximum. Descent can now be emulated on modern computers using dxx-rebirth (dxxrebirth.com) or d2x-xl (descent2.de).

There is Descent 1 (1994), Descent 2 (1996), and Descent 3 (1999). Then there were expansions such as Descent: Levels of the World, Descent 2: The Vertigo Series, Descent 2: The Infinite Abyss, and Descent 3: Mercenary.

You play as a mercenary for the PTMC (Post Terran Mining Company). The robots in the mines were hacked by an alien virus, and you must destroy them in your spaceship.

Descent 2 is about the mercenary realizing the PTMC is keeping him on retainer for 72 hours—meaning no pay yet and more dangerous work.

The warp drive glitches and radiation blasts the ship toward the sun while the mercenary passes out, unable to signal for help.

Rescued by the Red Acropolis team, the mercenary learns the virus was modified and his "glitch" was intentional. He gets a new ship to stop Dravis.

The mercenary broadcasts the anti-virus program, infiltrates Dravis's hideout, and Dravis ends up dying in the final fight.

Duke Nukem 3D only has 9 weapons, while Descent has 10 to 20+. Descent is also more kid-friendly as it lacks the inappropriate content found in Duke Nukem.

Doom has fewer weapons and can be less kid-friendly. (Note: This is an FAQ, not "frequently uttered statements"—security ops haven't been hired yet, so these words can stay!)

Descent 3 has a filter for strong language. You can buy modern versions of the games on Steam or GOG.com.
